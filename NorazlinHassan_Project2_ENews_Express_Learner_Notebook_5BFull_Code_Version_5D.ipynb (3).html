Project Business Statistics: E-news Express¶

Marks: 60

Define Problem Statement and Objectives¶

The executives at E-news Express are of the opinion that there has been a decline in new monthly subscribers compared to the past year because the current webpage is not designed well enough to make a customer subscribe to their services.

A new and improved landing page has been created by the design team.

The main objective now is to test the effectiveness of the new landing page in gathering new subscribers to E-news Express.

Data Dictionary:

An experiment has been conducted by randomly selecting 100 users and diving them equally into 2 groups:

  • The control group (50 users) were served with the old landing page.
  • The treatment group (50 users) were served with the new landing page.

The abtest.csv file contains the following information:

  1. user_id - Unique user ID of the person visiting the website
  2. group - Whether the user belongs to the first group (control) or the second group (treatment)
  3. landing_page - Whether the landing page is new or old
  4. time_spent_on_the_page - Time (in minutes) spent by the user on the landing page
  5. converted - Whether the user gets converted to a subscriber of the news portal or not
  6. language_preferred - Language chosen by the user to view the landing page

Specific questions to be answered:

  1. Do the users spend more time on the new landing page than on the existing landing page?
  2. Is the conversion rate (the proportion of users who visit the landing page and get converted) for the new page greater than the conversion rate for the old page?
  3. Does the converted status depend on the preferred language?
  4. Is the time spent on the new page the same for the different language users?

Solution Approach:

  • Explore the dataset and extract insights using Exploratory Data Analysis.
  • Perform statistical analysis, considering a significance level of 0.05 for all tests.
  • Provide conclusion and business recommendations.

1. Do the users spend more time on the new landing page than the existing landing page?¶

It looks like the treatment group spent more time viewing the new landing page compared to the control group viewing the old page.

Let's test if the observed difference of time spent is significant enough to conclude that users actually spent more time on the new landing page.

Step 1: Define the null and alternate hypotheses¶

Ho : Null Hypothesis is the mean time spent on the new landing page is the same as the mean time spent on the old landing page.

Ha : Alternate Hypothesis is the mean time spent on the new landing page is higher than the mean time spent on the old landing page.

Let 1 and 2 be the mean times spent on the new and old landing page, respectively.

Mathematically, the above formulated hypotheses can be written as:

Ho : 1 = 2

Ha : 1 > 2

Step 2: Select Appropriate test¶

Check the assumptions to determine the test to be used:

  • Continuous data - Yes, the time spent on landing pages is measured on a continuous scale.

  • Normally distributed populations - Looking at the histogram probability density curves, as well as considering larger sample sizes of 50 (>i.e., 30), normal distribution assumption holds.

  • Independent populations - As we are taking random samples from population for two different groups, the two samples are assumed to be independent.

  • Standard deviation is not given.

Based on the above assumptions, we will use the one-tailed 2-sample independent t-test.

Step 3: Decide the significance level¶

As given in the problem statement, significance level is 5%

= 0.05

Step 4: Collect and prepare data¶

As the p-value 0.0001392381225166549 is less than the level of significance, we reject the null hypothesis.

Step 7: Draw inference¶

Since the p-value is less than the level of significance, we reject the null hypothesis. Hence, we have enough evidence to support the claim that mean time spent on the new landing page is higher than mean time spent on the old landing page.

This is a one-tailed test concerning two population proportions from two independent populations. Therefore, we shall use the Proportion z-test.

Define the null and alternate hypotheses

Ho = Null hypothesis is the mean time spent on the new landing page by the three language users are the same.

Ha = Alternate hypothesis is that at least one of the mean time spent on the new landing page by the three languages is different than the rest.

Let 1 = 2 = 3 be the mean time spent on the new landing page of the English, French and Spanish language users.

Ho : 1 = 2 = 3

Ha : At least one of the 1, 2 or 3 is different from the rest.

Determine the appropriate test

This is a problem concerning three population means. One-way ANOVA is an appropriate test here provided normality and equality of variance assumptions are verified.

For testing of normality, Shapiro-Wilks test is applied to the response variable.

For equality of variance, Levene's test is applied to the response variable.

Shapiro-Wilk's test:

Ho: Time spent on the new landing page follows a normal distribution

Ha: Time spent on the new landing page does not follow a normal distribution

Conclusion and Business Recommendations¶

Conclusion:

Based on the exploratory data and statistical analyses, we can conclude that:

  1. Sample data shows overall users spent more time on the new landing page than on the old page. We found statistical evidence to support this claim.

  2. Sample data shows that the users who converted to becoming subscribers are likely those who spent longer time on the landing page.

  3. Consequently, data shows user-to-subscriber conversion rate is higher on the new landing page compared to the old page. This claim is also supported by statistical evidence.

  4. Converted status and preferred language are independent of one another. There is no statistical evidence to support the claim that converted status depend on the preferred language.

  5. Even though the data and visual analysis show the time spent on the new page by the three language users are different, the mean time spent are close to each other. Hence, we could not find significant statistical evidence to conclude that at least one of the mean time spent is different.

  6. Based on the findings above, the new landing page shows to be effective in engaging customers to spend more time on the news portal and gathering new subscribers.

Business Recommendations:

  1. E-news Express should roll out the new landing page to the rest of their customer base to replace the old page.

  2. Preferred language is the only portal UI/UX feature studied in this case and it shows no impact on the conversion rate.
    Therefore, we would like to recommended further investigation on which other features within the new landing page customers find valuable. This would be helpful for the design team and the company to provide better customer experience, as well as attract more subscribers in the long run.
